Q: Is there a better alternative to Endolift® in the UK?
A: Yes. Endolift® pioneered laser fibre lifting in the mid-2010s using a single 1470nm diode. Cosmedocs offers XL Endolaser — a second-generation dual-wavelength (980nm + 1470nm) laser fibre lift delivered under one 200µm micro-fibre, doctor-led on Harley Street. Same clinical principle, upgraded targeting, and typically longer results because deeper adipose remodelling reduces re-descent of overlying tissue.

Q: What is the difference between Endolift® (1G) and XL Endolaser (2G)?
A: Endolift® uses a single 1470nm wavelength targeting water and collagen. XL Endolaser combines 980nm (targeting adipose and vasculature) with 1470nm (targeting water and collagen) through the same optical fibre. One pass tightens skin AND remodels small fat pockets — especially useful for double chin, jowls and pre-jowl sulcus.

Q: How much does the Endolift® alternative cost at Cosmedocs?
A: XL Endolaser starts from £450 per zone. Full-face + neck packages are quoted at consultation. Endolift® in the UK typically ranges £1,500–£3,500 per zone.

Q: Is XL Endolaser regulated in the UK?
A: The device carries UKCA and CE marks. It is delivered doctor-led by Dr Ahmed Haq in partnership with a CQC-registered clinical facility (PrivaDr Ltd, 10 Harley Street, London W1G 9PF).

Q: Which lasts longer — Endolift® or XL Endolaser?
A: Both stimulate neocollagenesis; results build over 3–6 months. Endolift® typically lasts 12–18 months from a single session. Cosmedocs' clinical audit on XL Endolaser shows 18–24 months — marginally longer because 980nm adipose remodelling reduces soft-tissue re-descent.

Q: Can XL Endolaser treat a double chin better than Endolift®?
A: For a double chin with mild-to-moderate skin laxity, yes. 980nm addresses the sub-mental adipose pocket; 1470nm tightens the overlying skin. A single pass handles both. 1G Endolift® can improve the skin envelope but has limited effect on the underlying fat.

Q: Is it safe?
A: Both wavelengths have decades of published safety data (980nm from endovenous surgery, 1470nm from intra-dermal tightening). Downtime is typically 2–3 days. Common transient effects: swelling, small bruises, mild tenderness. Serious complications are rare when the fibre is placed doctor-led at the correct plane.

Q: How long is the appointment?
A: 45–90 minutes depending on zones. Local anaesthesia. Patient is awake.

Q: When will I see results?
A: Visible tightening from 3–6 weeks. Peak result at 3–6 months. Continued collagen remodelling for up to 12 months.

Q: Who is not a candidate?
A: Pregnancy, breastfeeding, active skin infection in the treatment zone, or advanced laxity requiring surgical lifting. Consultation confirms candidacy.
